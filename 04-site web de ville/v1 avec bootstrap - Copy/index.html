Historical monuments of Essaouira

Dar Sultan Palace

It is noteworthy that the Sultan House is considered one of the most important and prominent historical buildings that furnish the architectural and heritage specificity in the province of Essaouira. Important diplomatic figures and expatriates from foreign missions. This historic building is characterized by unique architectural features, symbolizing the richness of Moroccan architecture with its multiplicity of civilization and heritage components.

Tower El Berod

The Baroud Tower is a ruined old watchtower located on the outskirts of the city of Essaouira between Diabat and the island near the beach, located to the south of the valley of the reeds near Essaouira, and this historical landmark is located on a wide sandy beach on the other side of the Phoenician ruins at the southeastern tip of the Burburi Islands Main, and there are many reasons to believe that this structure may have been an integral part of an ancient Phoenician settlement, and it is one of the important archaeological landmarks of the city that you cannot miss to visit..

Old city

The city of Essaouira, or Mogador as it is known as Amazigh, is a Moroccan city overlooking the Atlantic Ocean. The old town of Essaouira is an exceptional example of a fortified city dating back to the late eighteenth century, and it was built according to the principles of contemporary European military architecture in North Africa, and since its foundation, Its port was a major international trade route linking Morocco and its desert lands with Europe and the rest of the world, and it joined the list of World Heritage Sites in Morocco in 2001, when it was registered within the UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Activities

Horseback riding on Essaouira beach

Ride horses through the beaches, dunes, and forest of Essaouira on a 2-hour guided tour on a well-trained mount. Select a horse that suits your riding experience - beginners start with a basic riding lesson - then walk, trot, or gallop on the soft sand; Climb steep dunes by the sea for panoramic views, and meander in the shade of the arid forests of Essaouira.

2 hours of off-road quad-biking on the beach and in the dunes

Get outside and experience an energizing quad adventure. You won't have to worry if you've never been on a quad bike before, as this tour starts in the village of Diabat to allow you to get used to riding your quad. By taking a guided tour, you will no longer have to worry about getting lost and you will discover distant landscapes that you might not see otherwise. Drive on the beach, climb the sand dunes and explore the vast forest.

Kite Surf

ideal place for kitesurfing all year round. In all seasons, the conditions allow the learning and practice of kitesurfing in many places in the region.

Sunset at the Sultans Palace in Diabat.

This one hour hike through the dunes and along the Oued Ksob will allow you to discover the richness of the natural environment of the surroundings of Essaouira. You will have the pleasure of discovering the silted fortress of a Sultan who sits in the middle of a wild nature. The huge palace dating from the founding of Essaouira in the 18th century is still dreaming.

The festivals

Fistival Gnaoua

The Essaouira Gnaoua and World Music Festival was created in 1997. The founders aimed to make it a story of encounters, mergers and openness to others2. This festival, produced by Neila Tazi, has set itself the central mission of preserving, enhancing and promoting Gnaoua music and culture in Morocco and around the world. Allow the Maâlems, custodians of a long musical and mystical tradition, to take the stage alongside universal artists and create unique musical moments.

Jazz festival under the Argan tree

In order to enchant travelers passing through Essaouira and those who have stayed in town for the Christmas holidays, this festival will end your year 2019 in style with eclectic sounds that showcase Africa in all its diversity and especially the richness of an often unrecognized Jazz. The Jazzmen will come from three continents, with European, Cuban, and of course African musicians.
